Trämli Apotheke

  • Summary

The “Trämli Apotheke” is a newly opened Pharmacy in Weil am Rhein. “Trämli” is the swiss expression for a streetcar, The owner wanted to refer to it, because his location is right next to a Tram Stop, which plays a big role in acquiring new customers for him.


By using the circle as a core element of the signet, I wanted to visualize health as a surrounding of our body as well as the geometrical shape of a tram stop sign. Before designing the outline of the streetcar, I researched for designs of historic streetcars in the city of Basle and tried to match the modern and clean style I wanted throughout the appearance of the logo.

  • Colors

For the logo I used the color of the streetcars in the area the pharmacy is located. As well the color connects to the greens which are used in all different medical institutions.

  • Typeface

The Typeface I chose for the Logo is Amsi Pro Narrow by Stawix Ruecha. The Font is inspired by the swiss classic Block Berthold Condensed with it’s modernistic look and feel. While developing the Logotype I tried to find a typeface that’s easy readable, even for people with limited vision or other restrictions of their eyesight.
